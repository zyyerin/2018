–––  identity

PastFuture Cafe serves as a critique on current usage of big data for decision making. Its name comes from an essay, Big Data for Development: From Information- to Knowledge Societies, by Martin Hilbert: the decisions based on the analysis of big data are inevitably informed by the world as it was in the past, or, at best, as it currently is. It aims at raising customers’ awareness of the danger of big data through the experience at the cafe.

Notice this project focus on the visual aspect of the cafe, rather than the entire service. Also, I used an analog approach for this project, drawing and making with hand instead of getting things done digitally.

Draft

I started to work on the cafe’s logo and wordmark after decided its topic and name. Using an hourglass as a symbol of time came to mind naturally, and I settled on the idea of indicating a transition from digital to physical by mixing pixel with concrete lines.

Wordmark

The wordmark, however, takes quite awhile. I tested many typefaces and used all sorts of ways to mix types and combine the words to make something interesting and meaningful.

PATTERN

Since the cafe is a critique, I chose broken glass as the pattern for the system. I got some glass screen shields, broke them and photographed(sometimes scanned) those pieces in various ways. Glass as a material is used throughout the identity system.

ICONS

I have a clear idea of having the icons on the cafe’s website, so most of the icons are straightforward.

WIREFRAME

The cafe’s website is a main component of its service. It’s a platform where people can access data be collected during their stay at the cafe, and allows them to edit and store the data. It reveals the data collection happened without one’s awareness as well as points out the unreliability of the source.

DELIVERABLES

LOGOMARK & STYLE GUIDE

This is a minimal style guide showing only the type choices and palette. The color is less of a concern in this system, for the materiality of glass is emphasized.

SPACE

I want the cafe to have a warm feeling, to create the contrast with the idea of big data. You should feel happy inside the cafe while enjoying the food and drink. Yet in the end, customers will be more aware of the issue as they leave. Designing the service in the cafe will have a major role if the project develops.
